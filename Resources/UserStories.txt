Functional Requirement User Stories:
1. As a patient, I want my insulin pump to accurately maintain my glucose levels, so that I can stay healthy. 
2. As a patient, I want basal insulin to be continously delievered over 24 hours, so that I don't have to use an insulin injection pen. 
3. As a patient, I want to be able to program how much bolus insulin I need, so that the insulin pump will deliver it to cover the carbohydrates in each of my meals or snacks. 
4. As a patient, I want to be able to program the insulin pump to make correctional or supplemental doses when I ask it to, so that I don't have to use an insulin injection pen.
5. As a patient, I want to be able to control the amount of insulin given to me by the pump before and after I exercise, so that I do not risk hypoglycemia. 
6. As a patient, I want the product to stop administering insulin if I remove the pump so that insulin is not wasted, and I am not over-medicated when I reattach the pump.
7. As a patient, I want the product to display a message, so that I am aware of what is going on with my glucose levels.
8. As a patient, I want to be notified when my insulin levels in the pump are low, so that I know when to replenish it.  
9. As a patient, I want the product to contact emergency services if I am unable to respond to warning messages, so that my life will be saved.
10. As a patient, I want the ones who care about me to be notified via an alert system when I am in trouble, so they may assist me. 
11. As a patient, I want my data to have the option of being uploaded online, so that I can access the information with ease and track it at any time.
12. As a doctor, I want to be able to access my patient's insulin pump information, so that I can ensure his/her safety and instruct my patient on better use of the pump.
13. As a patient, I want child-lock options for my insulin pump so that my children are not able to mess with it and harm themselves or myself doing so.
14.As a patient, I want to be able to tell the pump if I have administered a certain amount of insulin to myself by other means than the pump so that the pump does not give me too much insulin.
15. As a patient, I want the product to regulate the temperature of the insulin so that it does not go bad due to having too high or too low of a temperature. 

Non-Functional Requirement User Stories:
1. As an administrator, I want this project to be coded in Java and JavaFX, so my team can maintain it easily.
2. As a user, I want this machine to maintain my blood sugar, or else I am in risk of death.
3. As an administrator, I want this project's database to be coded in MySQL, because my team can maintain it easier.
